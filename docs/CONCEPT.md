# Chai Empire

## High Concept
An offline idle-upgrades mobile game for Indian audiences where the player starts with a tiny roadside chai stall and grows it into a thriving chai empire. Customers spawn automatically. The player improves speed, menu, staff, quality, pricing, and shop prestige, then upgrades the business from a stall to a kiosk, to a small shop, to a full cafe, and eventually a local chain.

## Core Promise
Simple, satisfying, highly replayable business growth with a deeply Indian setting.

## Genre Positioning
- idle / incremental game
- business simulation
- light tycoon
- short-session mobile game
- offline-first

## Audience Fit for India
Why this works:
- chai is universal and instantly understood across India
- low learning curve, high relatability
- suitable for lower-end devices if built efficiently
- familiar aspiration arc: small start, steady hustle, visible growth
- rich room for Indian flavor without heavy art production

## Research-Informed Design Direction
This concept draws from proven patterns in:
- incremental/idle games: automation, frequent upgrades, milestone rewards, offline progress
- business sims like Lemonade Stand: pricing, stock, daily conditions, demand shifts
- time-management progression like Diner Dash: service speed, queue pressure, satisfaction feedback

This game should not copy those directly. Instead, it should combine:
- automatic customer arrival and sales flow
- active upgrade choices with visible impact
- India-specific setting, items, events, and progression fantasy

## Core Gameplay Loop
1. Customers arrive automatically.
2. Tea and unlocked items are prepared and sold automatically based on worker count and serving speed.
3. Player spends earnings on upgrades.
4. Upgrades improve throughput, quality, menu variety, profit per order, and customer happiness.
5. Once a venue is maxed enough, the player unlocks the next business tier.
6. Progress continues through larger venues, more complex service, and stronger automation.

## Starting Experience
You begin with:
- one roadside chai stall
- one kettle
- one basic tea item
- one serving worker, often the owner
- slow serving speed
- low customer capacity

Early upgrades:
- faster chai brewing
- better stove
- more cups in reserve
- second helper
- better signage
- improved milk and masala quality

## Venue Progression
1. Roadside Stall
2. Pushcart Kiosk
3. Small Shop
4. Busy Corner Tea Shop
5. Neighborhood Cafe
6. Premium Chai Lounge
7. Mini City Chain

Each venue unlocks:
- higher customer volume
- new item categories
- higher revenue ceilings
- new staff slots
- better décor/status boosts

## Endless Design
This should feel endless through layered progression:
- venue upgrades and expansions
- item unlock trees
- automation depth
- city map expansion later
- prestige mechanic later, for example: "Legacy Recipe" reset bonus

For v1, endless means there is no hard ending. The player keeps scaling the business and unlocking better venues.
