# Chai Empire Brand System

## Brand Positioning
A warm, aspirational idle tycoon where a humble chai tapri grows into a beloved national brand.

## Core Brand Pillars
- Familiar: built around a daily ritual almost every player instantly understands
- Hopeful: small beginnings, steady progress, visible success
- Cozy: steam, rain, rush hour, bun maska, kulhad charm
- Broad-market: India-native without depending on narrow regional slang

## Name Direction
### Master Brand
- **Chai Empire**

Why it works:
- easy to read
- ambitious and memorable
- clearly signals progression fantasy

## Tagline Options
### Best options
- **Start with one kettle. Build a chai legend.**
- **From tapri to town favorite.**
- **Brew fast. Grow big. Rule the rush.**

### Alternate options
- Start small. Serve hot. Dream bigger.
- One stall. Endless chai. Big ambitions.
- Turn daily chai into a booming business.
- Build the busiest chai spot in town.

## Venue Naming Style
Use names that feel like growth, not just bigger buildings.

### Recommended progression
1. **Tapri Start**
2. **Bazaar Cart**
3. **Corner Stall**
4. **Mohalla Chai Shop**
5. **City Adda**
6. **Kulhad Cafe**
7. **Chai Empire**

## Naming Notes
- Prefer 2 to 3 words
- Keep one familiar anchor word like chai, adda, bazaar, kulhad, tapri
- Avoid heavy Hinglish in core UI
- Let flavor text carry personality, not the main labels

## Tutorial Voice
### Voice rules
- short
- warm
- clear on first read
- mildly celebratory
- never bossy or complicated

### Voice sample
"Good start. The kettle is on, customers are coming, and your stall is open for business."

## Delight Layer
Use tiny bits of local flavor in reactions and milestone moments.
Examples:
- office rush is picking up
- perfect weather for chai
- your stall is becoming the local adda
- that kulhad upgrade looks classy

## Avoid
- niche memes
- hard-to-translate slang
- mean or overly sarcastic tutorial tone
- text that sounds stressful or punitive
